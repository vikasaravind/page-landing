The Chardham Yatra is a significant Hindu pilgrimage that involves visiting four sacred shrines in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. The four shrines, collectively known as the "Chota Char Dham"

Yamunotri

Yamunotri is located in the Garhwal Himalayas, in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand. Situated in the Garhwal Himalayas, at an elevation of approximately 3,293 meters (10,804 feet). Dedicated To Goddess Yamuna.Significance of the temple marks the origin of the Yamuna River. Pilgrims visit the temple and the nearby thermal springs, such as Surya Kund. The journey typically starts from the town of Hanuman Chatti, followed by a trek or pony ride to the temple. The temple was originally constructed by Maharani Guleria of Jaipur in the 19th century and has undergone several reconstructions due to natural calamities. Built with granite stones, the temple has a simple and serene architecture typical of Himalayan temples. It marks the source of the Yamuna River, which is considered sacred in Hinduism. Pilgrims visit the temple to seek the blessings of Goddess Yamuna and to purify themselves in the holy waters.
